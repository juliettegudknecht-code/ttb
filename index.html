TTB by the Numbers

The alcohol, tobacco, firearms, and ammunition excise taxes collected by the Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau, and the industries that pay them. Fiscal year 2015 through fiscal year 2025, built entirely from TTB's published data.

$0.0 billion collected in FY 2025 across all four excise taxes, imports included

TTB is the Treasury bureau that collects federal excise taxes on alcohol, tobacco, firearms, and ammunition, and regulates the industries that pay them. Collections were $20.6 billion in FY 2025, down from $25.5 billion in FY 2015. That is a fall of about 19 percent in a decade, and one product accounts for nearly all of it.

This page is a working sample: named sources on every figure, definitions reconciled where TTB reports differ, and redacted values shown as gaps rather than estimates. It is an independent presentation of public data and is not affiliated with TTB or the Treasury.

Collections over time

TTB reports two collection totals, and both are correct. The all-in total counts import excise collected at the border by U.S. Customs and Border Protection; the domestic total counts only what TTB administers itself. The two lines diverge as imports grow, from 13 percent of the total in FY 2015 to 25 percent in FY 2025.

Where FY 2025 collections came from

Alcohol together has outweighed tobacco every fiscal year since FY 2022, led by distilled spirits. Firearms and ammunition is the smallest of the four and the only one that is earmarked: under the Pittman-Robertson Act of 1937, all of it funds wildlife restoration.

Alcohol production

TTB's operational reports track what the country actually makes and sends to the taxed market. Beer removals fell 21 percent from 2015 to 2025. Wine production dropped from its 2017 peak of 890 million gallons to 559 million. Whisky production more than doubled through 2023, then gave back nearly a third in two years.

On the 2025 spirits gap: TTB redacts the 2025 national total for distilled-spirits taxable withdrawals to avoid disclosing proprietary data. It appears here as a break in the line, not an estimate. Nothing redacted on this page was imputed.

Tobacco, the shrinking base

Tobacco built this tax base and is now shrinking it. Collections fell from $14.5 billion in FY 2015 to $8.9 billion in FY 2025, a $5.5 billion drop that outweighs TTB's entire net decline; rising spirits collections offset part of it. The federal rate has held at $1.01 a pack since 2009, so the fall is volume: taxed cigarettes more than halved after 2012.

The regulated industry

Volumes fell, but the number of licensed producers climbed. A TTB permit is the price of entry, and entry boomed: registered breweries are up sixfold since 2010, distillery permits more than fivefold since 2012, and bonded wine producers roughly doubled.

Permit counts and producer counts differ, and both are right. Permits count every premises authorized to operate; production charts count only those that filed reports that year. In 2024, 15,166 breweries were registered and 7,888 filed reports.

Notes on the data

  • Sources. Collections come from TTB Statistical Release TTB S 5630. Production and removals come from the TTB National Statistical Reports for beer (S 5130), wine (S 5120), distilled spirits (S 5110), and tobacco. Permit counts come from TTB National Revenue Center reports and the Distilled Spirits Permit Counts report. All files are current as of late June 2026, from ttb.gov/data.
  • Two totals. Collections come from statistical releases TTB labels unofficial; official revenue appears in the annual report. The all-in total includes import excise collected by U.S. Customs and Border Protection; the domestic total does not.
  • Years differ by dataset. Collections use the federal fiscal year, October through September. Production, removals, and permit counts use calendar years.
  • Units. A barrel of beer is 31 gallons. Wine is measured in wine gallons; spirits in proof gallons. Dollar figures are nominal and not adjusted for inflation.
  • Suppression. Where a national total would reveal proprietary data, TTB redacts it. The 2025 spirits taxable-withdrawals total is redacted and shown as a gap. No redacted value here was estimated.
  • Coverage. Brewery and bonded-wine counts cover the continental United States only. TTB publishes no volume by brand or company, so none appears here.
